<div class="statute-body">
  <p class="block subsection">(1) The Authority may specify an information security policy setting out inter alia the technical and organisational measures to be adopted by the Authority and its personnel, and also security measures to be adopted by agencies, advisors, consultants and other service providers engaged by the Authority, registrar, enrolling agency, requesting entities, and Authentication Service Agencies. ¹Hkkx IIIµ[k.M 4º Hkkjr dk jkti=k % vlk/kj.k 71</p>
  <p class="block subsection">(2) Such information security policy may provide for:—</p>
  <p class="block clause">(a) identifying and maintaining an inventory of assets associated with the information and information processing facilities;</p>
  <p class="block clause">(b) implementing controls to prevent and detect any loss, damage, theft or compromise of the assets;</p>
  <p class="block clause">(c) allowing only controlled access to confidential information;</p>
  <p class="block clause">(d) implementing controls to detect and protect against virus/malwares;</p>
  <p class="block clause">(e) a change management process to ensure information security is maintained during changes;</p>
  <p class="block clause">(f) a patch management process to protect information systems from vulnerabilities and security risks;</p>
  <p class="block clause">(g) a robust monitoring process to identify unusual events and patterns that could impact security and performance of information systems and a proper reporting and mitigation process;</p>
  <p class="block clause">(h) encryption of data packets containing biometrics, and enabling decryption only in secured locations;</p>
  <p class="block clause">(i) partitioning of CIDR network into zones based on risk and trust;</p>
  <p class="block clause">(j) deploying necessary technical controls for protecting CIDR network;</p>
  <p class="block clause">(k) service continuity in case of a disaster;</p>
  <p class="block clause">(l) monitoring of equipment, systems and networks;</p>
  <p class="block clause">(m) measures for fraud prevention and effective remedies in case of fraud;</p>
  <p class="block clause">(n) requirement of entering into non-disclosure agreements with the personnel;</p>
  <p class="block clause">(o) provisions for audit of internal systems and networks;</p>
  <p class="block clause">(p) restrictions on personnel relating to processes, systems and networks.</p>
  <p class="block clause">(q) inclusion of security and confidentiality obligations in the agreements or arrangements with the agencies, consultants, advisors or other persons engaged by the Authority.</p>
  <p class="block subsection">(3) The Authority shall monitor compliance with the information security policy and other security requirements through internal audits or through independent agencies.</p>
  <p class="block subsection">(4) The Authority shall designate an officer as Chief Information Security Officer for disseminating and monitoring the information security policy and other security-related programmes and initiatives of the Authority. 4. Security obligations of the personnel —</p>
  <p class="block subsection">(1) The personnel shall comply with the information security policy, and other policies, guidelines, procedures, etc. issued by the Authority from time to time.</p>
  <p class="block subsection">(2) Without prejudice to any action that may be taken under the Act, personnel may be liable to action in accordance with procedures specified by the Authority for this purpose:</p>
  <p class="block proviso">Provided that no such action shall be taken without giving the concerned personnel a reasonable opportunity of being heard.</p>
</div>
